What is WHR?

The waist-to-hip ratio is a measurement that compares the circumference of the waist to that of the hips. It is often used as an indicator of overall health and body composition. A higher waist-to-hip ratio is associated with increased risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and stroke. This is because excess fat around the waist, known as visceral fat, can be more harmful to health than fat stored in other areas of the body.
